Everything You Need to Know

Yes, NRIs can invest in Indian mutual funds by opening either an NRE or NRO account and completing the mandatory KYC process as per FEMA and SEBI regulations.
Required documents include a PAN card, proof of overseas address, a valid passport, visa or OCI/PIO card, and bank details (NRE/NRO).
NRE accounts are ideal for investments that you want to repatriate freely, while NRO accounts suit income earned or held in India; NRO is non-repatriable except up to USD 1 million per financial year.
Yes, new NRIs must update their KYC with overseas address proof and relevant documents once their residency status changes.
Yes, NRIs can start or continue SIPs in Indian mutual funds provided their accounts and KYC are updated with NRI status and proper bank details.
Returns are subject to currency risk—when converting INR returns to another currency (USD, GBP, AED), fluctuations can affect actual gains.
DTAA is an agreement between India and other countries to avoid double taxation. NRIs can avail treaty benefits by submitting relevant forms and proof of residence.
