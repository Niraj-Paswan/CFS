The cost of these courses varies significantly based on the type of institution. For a full degree, the approximate cost ranges are: Engineering: A degree from a government college can cost from ₹2 lakhs, while a private institution can range from ₹5 to over ₹15 lakhs. Medical (MBBS): A government college can cost between ₹2-10 lakhs, while a private college can range from ₹40 lakhs to over ₹1 crore. MBA: Costs for an MBA from a government or lesser popular B-school can range from ₹2-10 lakhs, while an MBA from IIM or other B-schools colleges can cost from ₹15 to over ₹30 lakhs.
The cost of higher education typically rises faster than the general inflation rate. This is known as educational inflation. When planning for your child’s future education, it is essential to factor in this higher inflation rate to ensure your savings keep pace and you are not left with a significant funding gap
Inflation can quietly erode the value of your savings. A common strategy to combat this is to invest in instruments like mutual funds that have the potential to earn returns that are higher than the inflation rate, ensuring your money's purchasing power grows over time.
Estimating the future cost requires considering the current cost of the desired education and then applying an assumed rate of educational inflation over the number of years until your child begins college. Financial planning tools and calculators can help you with this projection. A financial advisor can also provide a more precise calculation and help you build a plan to meet that target.
