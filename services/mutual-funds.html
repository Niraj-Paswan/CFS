The Peace of Mind You Deserve

Investing in mutual funds can feel intimidating, but it doesn't have to be. We help you find peace of mind by providing clarity on the core benefits that come with this investment vehicle.

Professional Management

Your money is managed by experienced fund managers who research and select securities for your portfolio. This expertise helps you navigate market complexities without needing to be an expert yourself.

Diversification

A mutual fund invests in a basket of securities, automatically spreading your risk across different assets and sectors. This strategy helps to reduce the impact of a poor performance by any single investment.

Low-Cost Investing

Mutual funds offer a cost-effective way to access professional management. The expense ratio i.e. management costs are typically much lower than many other investment options such as portfolio management services (PMS).

Simplicity of SIP

A Systematic Investment Plan allows you to invest a fixed amount regularly, helping you build discipline and benefit from rupee cost averaging. It turns small, consistent contributions into a powerful long-term wealth-building habit.

Liquidity

You can easily redeem (liquidate) units of open ended mutual fund schemes to meet your financial needs on any business day (when the stock markets and/or banks are open), so you have easy access to your money.

Tax benefits

Investment in ELSS mutual funds upto ₹1,50,000 annually qualifies for tax benefit under section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961. Mutual Fund investments when held for a longer term are tax efficient compared to others like FDs,

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Need to Know

A mutual fund is an investment vehicle that pools money from a large number of investors to invest in a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities. This allows investors to access a variety of assets under the management of a professional fund manager, who makes investment decisions on behalf of all the investors.
Mutual funds are primarily categorized based on the assets they invest in. Equity funds invest mainly in stocks, aiming for long-term growth. Debt funds invest in fixed-income securities like bonds, focusing on stability. Hybrid funds invest in a mix of both, balancing growth and stability. There are many sub-categories within these main types.
A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing a fixed amount of money at regular intervals, such as every month. A lump-sum investment is a one-time, single investment of a large sum. A SIP helps build investment discipline and benefits from rupee-cost averaging, while a lump-sum is suitable when you have a significant amount of money to invest at once
Mutual funds are subject to market risk, meaning their value can fluctuate based on market performance. However, they are highly regulated by SEBI in India, ensuring transparency and investor protection. Your investments are held in your name, and funds are managed by professional fund managers.
Mutual funds do not pay interest. Instead, they generate returns based on the performance of the underlying assets. These returns are not fixed or guaranteed and can fluctuate (go up and down) in value. They come in the form of an increase in the fund’s Net Asset Value (NAV) and, in some cases, dividends.
Mutual funds and Fixed Deposits (FDs) serve different financial purposes. FDs offer fixed, guaranteed returns and capital protection but also incur income tax. Mutual funds offer the potential for higher but variable returns, are subject to market risk and taxed as capital gains. The "better" option depends entirely on your specific financial goals and risk tolerance.
Mutual fund returns are taxed as capital gains. The tax treatment depends on the type of fund (equity or debt) and the holding period of your investment. It is essential to understand the tax implications of your investments, and for personal advice, it is always recommended to consult a professional for guidance.
Choosing a mutual fund should be aligned with your personal financial goals and risk tolerance. It is important to first define your objective (e.g., long-term growth, stability, tax saving). You should also consider the fund’s objective, historical performance, and the fund manager's track record before making a decision.
Most mutual funds are open-ended, which means you can withdraw your money at any time. However, some funds may have a lock in period (like ELSS funds for tax saving) or an exit load if you redeem your investment before a specific period, which should be checked beforehand.
It is crucial to stay calm and avoid making impulsive decisions. Review your original investment goals and the timeline you set. If the fund's fundamentals are strong and your goal is long-term, staying invested through market volatility is often the best strategy.
